Writing CSS almost always entails an element of design. Even if you're interpreting a comp from a designer into CSS, you're going to run into situations where a design  judgement call needs to be made.

Translating web design to print: literal translation doesn't always make sense.

Also, bear in mind that most home printers impose a mandatory un-printable margin on every page. This is space that the printer needs to pull the paper through the machinery, and can't be printed on. Commercial printers generally trim pages, to allow for full bleeds. When including bleeds in a design, keep in mind who will be printing and what kind of equipment they have. Try to make your design work for as many people as possible (unless you are making an artistic statement, and then by all means follow your heart. Art is not dead.)

Responsive design: styling for print can be thought of as another piece of responsive design. Create once, print everywhere.
